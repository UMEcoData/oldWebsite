Daily Usage Water Calculator

Wenqian Li, Emma Thronson, Joan Suh, Sydney Finkelstein, Shruthi Chakravarthy, Edwin Chan, Jennifer Lee, Miranda Baumbick

Motivation:

Water shortage has been a critical and ongoing issue for a while. However, with everything going on in our lives—whether it be school, work or other personal occupancies—it is easy to be oblivious to, or take, our water usage for granted. Thus, through this project we hope to give an estimate of how much water we use on a daily basis—these estimated usages have been calculated based on an annual scale to keep them uniform. It might be alarming to see how much water we roughly use each year, but we hope that these values encourage all of us to become more conscious of our actions.



Methods & Findings:

Our initial idea was to create a water calculator application which calculated the water usage for the creation of household items used daily. We then did research, browsing various internet sources to find data on how much water in gallons was used in the production and each use of a product (toilet paper, toilet usage, bottles, etc.). However, finding data for the water usage of the production of items proved to be difficult since there was not enough information for certain products. We then decided to change our project goal to calculating both the water usage of the creation or the usage of household items used daily. This semester, we focused on calculating the estimated annual bathroom water usage in gallons of common bathroom products and facilities. We used HTML to produce our application, requesting the user for information about how many times they flush, how many bottles of shampoo/conditioner they use, and how many rolls of toilet paper they use. Using the user’s inputs, we then calculated the total estimated amount of gallons of water used annually and displayed it to the user.
